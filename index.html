How to Use the TradeRisk Calculator

1. Enter your Account Capital

This is your total trading balance. For example, if you have $1,000, type 1000 in the field.

2. Set your Risk % per trade

How much of your account you are willing to risk. Recommended: 1%–2%. For example, 2% of a $1,000 account = $20 risk.

3. Enter Stop-Loss %

Find your Stop-Loss percentage in TradingView’s Short & Long Position Tools.

4. Set Margin Type

Ensure your Margin Order Size is set to USDT. Leverage doesn’t matter; position sizing does.

5. Review Results

The calculator displays your Total Margin and Risk Amount.

6. Place Your Order

Use the calculated margin and risk amount to place your trade safely.

7. Conclusion

The TradeRisk Calculator helps you find the right position size and manage your risk effectively.

You can’t control the market, but you can control your losses.
